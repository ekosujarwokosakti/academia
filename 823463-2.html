Introduction

Maritime pollution is a pressing environmental issue affecting oceans globally. Oil spills, plastic debris, and chemical discharges disrupt marine ecosystems and threaten biodiversity. Information systems have emerged as vital tools to monitor, analyze, and mitigate the effects of maritime pollution. This bibliography reviews key studies highlighting the role of information systems in tracking, preventing, and managing maritime pollution, focusing on oil spills and plastic pollution.

Analysis of Key Studies

Chen et al. (2019)

Reference: Chen, J., Zhang, W., Wan, Z., Li, S., Huang, T., & Fei, Y. (2019). Oil spills from global tankers: Status review and future governance. Journal of Cleaner Production, 227, 20–32. DOI

Chen et al. (2019) discuss oil spills as a major pollutant and the role of governance in managing them. Oil is critical to the global economy, and uneven resource distribution necessitates maritime transportation. Unsafe transport leads to oil spills, damaging oceans worldwide. The authors review historical oil trade, spill incidents, and preventive strategies. Their findings are essential for using information systems to develop strategies to reduce oil spill pollution.

Jepsen & de Bruyn (2019)

Reference: Jepsen, E. M., & de Bruyn, P. N. (2019). Pinniped entanglement in oceanic plastic pollution: A global review. Marine Pollution Bulletin, 145, 295–305. DOI

Jepsen and de Bruyn (2019) examine plastic pollution and its threat to marine mammals. They utilize four decades of database records to identify affected areas and the most endangered species. The study highlights reporting biases and insufficient awareness of plastic pollution. The research emphasizes information systems’ role in mapping pollution and developing targeted mitigation strategies.

Martinelli & Moroni (2018)

Reference: Martinelli, M., & Moroni, D. (2018). Volunteered geographic information for enhanced marine environment monitoring. Applied Sciences, 8(10), 1743. DOI

This study underscores the importance of remote sensing and geographic information systems (GIS) in monitoring maritime pollution, especially oil spills. Martinelli and Moroni propose a framework for collecting, disseminating, and analyzing environmental data. Their work demonstrates how information systems support accurate detection and mitigation of marine pollution.

Moroni et al. (2015)

Reference: Moroni, D., Pieri, G., Salvetti, O., & Tampucci, M. (2015, May). Proactive marine information system for environmental monitoring. In OCEANS 2015-Genova (pp. 1–5). IEEE. DOI

Moroni et al. (2015) focus on proactive monitoring of oil spills using integrated marine information systems. Their approach combines sensing technologies and interoperable communication systems to detect and manage maritime pollution efficiently, supporting timely interventions and reduced environmental damage.

Rezki et al. (2018)

Reference: Rezki, C. T., Soesilo, T. E. B., Herdiansyah, H., & Syahnoedi, U. (2018). Integrated hydrodynamic and oil spill modeling using OILMAP software for environment protection of oil spill in Cilacap Regency. In E3S Web of Conferences, 73, 03028. DOI

Rezki et al. (2018) integrate hydrodynamic modeling and GIS to assess oil spill impacts and support mitigation strategies. Their work illustrates how software tools provide real-time data for monitoring spills and managing environmental risks, demonstrating the practical applications of information systems in maritime pollution control.

Conclusion

The reviewed studies collectively show the crucial role of information systems in monitoring and mitigating maritime pollution. Technologies like GIS, remote sensing, and modeling software allow for timely detection, better governance, and strategic interventions. Implementing these systems helps protect marine ecosystems, supports sustainable maritime operations, and mitigates the effects of oil and plastic pollution.
